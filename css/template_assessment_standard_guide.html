Discussion Rubric (5-Point Scale)

Purpose: Weekly discussions assess students' ability to integrate mathematical thinking with Biblical worldview, communicate mathematical ideas clearly, and engage respectfully in collaborative discourse.

Criteria Excellent (5.0) Proficient (4.4) Developing (3.9) Needs Improvement (3.5) Insufficient (3.0)
Participation Asked or responded 6+ times with thoughtful engagement Asked one question; responded to two questions meaningfully Asked or responded to two questions Asked one question or provided one response Provided one minimal response
Quality of Math Content High-quality math content in thoughtful, engaging way; deep understanding Math-related suggestions in meaningful way; solid understanding Math-related suggestions; some understanding Little math content; limited understanding Not math-related; no understanding
Biblical Integration Deeply connected to Scripture, God's character, or Kingdom Domain; theological reflection Meaningful Biblical connection with relevant Scripture; discussed God's design Some Biblical reference or Christian perspective; surface-level connection One scriptural reference or minimal Christian perspective; connection felt added-on Lacked Biblical integration; no scriptural reference
Respectful Discourse Grace, humility, respect; built on others' ideas; Christ-like communication Respectful engagement; acknowledged others' contributions Mostly respectful tone; some acknowledgment of others Neutral tone; minimal acknowledgment of others Disrespectful, dismissive, or off-topic

Scoring: Average the four criteria scores to determine overall discussion grade.

Example: (5.0 + 4.4 + 3.9 + 4.4) ÷ 4 = 4.425 ≈ 4.4 (Proficient)

Grading Notes: Discussions due Friday each week. Students choose one of three prompts per week. Graded on engagement and understanding, not perfection.

Notebook Check Rubric (5-Point Scale)

Purpose: Weekly notebook checks assess students' organization, accuracy, and depth of mathematical work from CK-12 activities and independent practice.

Criteria Excellent (5.0) Proficient (4.4) Developing (3.9) Needs Improvement (3.5) Insufficient (3.0)
Completeness Full page of organized notes; all problems fully worked with steps shown 4+ fully-worked problems; most steps included Less than 4 problems fully worked; or 3 or fewer partially worked Less than 3 problems worked out; incomplete work Minimal notes; most work missing
Neatness & Organization Very neat writing; organized with clear labels and headings; easy to follow Clear writing; organized with some labels; generally easy to follow Confusing but legible; some organization; labels missing Confusing writing; minimal organization; hard to follow Very confusing; illegible; no organization
Accuracy & Understanding Deep understanding shown; no computational errors; accurate process Generally accurate; almost no errors; solid understanding Some understanding; a few computational errors; some inaccuracy Limited understanding; several mistakes and errors Very little understanding; many computational mistakes

Scoring: Average the three criteria scores to determine overall notebook grade.

Example: (4.4 + 4.4 + 3.9) ÷ 3 = 4.23 ≈ 4.4 (Proficient)

Grading Notes: Notebooks due Friday each week. Students submit physical notebooks or digital photos. Include work from CK-12 activities, sketches, calculations, and reflections. Grading focuses on process and understanding.

PBL Project Rubric (5-Point Scale)

Purpose: Quarterly PBL projects assess students' ability to apply geometric concepts to real-world contexts, integrate Biblical worldview, communicate findings clearly, and engage in peer review.

Scoring Criteria

  • Completeness: All required content complete; well-developed (Proficient 4.4)
  • Clarity & Organization: Content organized and makes sense; many labels added to diagrams; clear structure (Proficient 4.4)
  • Conceptual Understanding: Shows general understanding of concepts; solid mathematical reasoning (Proficient 4.4)
  • Computational Accuracy: Almost no computational errors; work is generally accurate (Proficient 4.4)
  • Peer Review Engagement: Two reviews given and received; meaningful revisions made (Proficient 4.4)
  • Kingdom Integration: Includes meaningful Biblical connections with relevant Scripture; discusses God's design in creation or human responsibility as image-bearers (Proficient 4.4)

Scoring: Average the six criteria scores to determine overall project grade.

Example: (4.4 + 4.4 + 4.4 + 4.4 + 4.4 + 4.4) ÷ 6 = 4.4 (Proficient)
