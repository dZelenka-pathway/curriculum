Instructions

Over the next 9 weeks, you will...:

1. Research (Weeks 1-3): Investigate the scientific concepts behind your lab topic. Explore relevant phenomena, prior experiments, and real-world applications.

2. Experiment & Data Collection (Weeks 4-5): Design and conduct your lab experiment. Record observations, measurements, and results using appropriate scientific tools and methods.

3. Analysis & Conclusion (Weeks 6-7): Analyze your data using graphs, tables, or models. Draw conclusions, identify sources of error, and connect findings to your hypothesis.

4. Peer Review & Revision (Week 8): Exchange projects with two classmates. Provide constructive feedback using the peer review template. Revise your work based on feedback received.

Groups: You may work individually or in pairs. If working in a pair, clearly identify which components each person completed.

Submission Format

  • Data/analysis section with labeled graphs, tables, or diagrams
  • Conclusion section addressing your hypothesis and real-world implications
  • Peer review section with feedback received and revisions made
  • Reflection: 1-2 paragraphs on how this project deepened your understanding of science and God's design in creation
