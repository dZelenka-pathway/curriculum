Course Purpose & Philosophy

[Purpose/objective description]

Through adaptive CK-12 practice, collaborative discussions rooted in Biblical worldview, hands-on investigations, and project-based learning, students will grow as mathematical thinkers and Christ-centered stewards of knowledge who see geometry not as abstract theory but as a window into God's creative genius.

Biblical Worldview Integration

All instruction intentionally integrates Pathway's Six Kingdom Domains:

  • Cognitive (COG): Understanding mathematical truth as reflection of God's eternal nature, logical order, and infinite wisdom
  • Affective (AFF): Developing wonder and worship through geometric beauty in creation; appreciating proportional harmony and symmetry
  • Behavioral (BEH): Practicing precision, perseverance, and intellectual integrity in proofs and problem-solving; disciplining the mind
  • Relational (REL): Collaborating in mathematical discourse with grace and humility; peer review reflecting covenant relationships
  • Missional (MIS): Applying geometric thinking to serve others and solve real-world problems; stewardship of knowledge and resources
  • Spiritual (SPI): Encountering God's infinite wisdom through mathematical patterns; seeing geometry as revelation of divine character

Integration Principle: Kingdom Domain integration must align naturally with academic objectives and be developmentally appropriate. Spiritual formation is coherent and instructionally sound, never decorative.

Course Design Principles

  1. Process-Centered Learning: Students engage in mathematical thinking through exploration, conjecture, proof, and application—not passive reception of facts.
  2. Biblical Integration: Every unit intentionally connects geometric concepts to God's character, creation, and purposes. Integration is organic, not decorative.
  3. Adaptive Mastery: CK-12 adaptive activities adjust to student pace and understanding. Students practice until competent, not on a fixed schedule.
  4. Collaborative Discourse: Discussions and peer review build mathematical communication skills and relational growth. Students learn from and with each other.
  5. Real-World Application: PBL projects connect geometry to authentic contexts: architecture, nature, design, culture. Mathematics serves human flourishing.
